David Dobrik Net Worth: How Rich Is He? Lifestyle And Career Highlights

May 2024 · 4 minute read

As of September 2023, David Dobrik has a net worth of $20 million and is a Slovakian-American actor, social media personality, and YouTuber. Dobrik is well known for his (now defunct) Vine and YouTube accounts. Furthermore, he is one of the most well-known and well-paid social media stars in the world, with annual profits recently exceeding $15 million.

David has over 45 million followers across all of his social media accounts as of this writing. He is claimed to have first achieved fame on YouTube before going on to TikTok. He is also well known as the “leader” of the well-known YouTube group The Vlog Squad. Not to note, Dobrik was included in the Forbes 30 Under 30 list in the social media category in December 2020. Forbes named Dobrik one of the highest-paid YouTube stars in 2020.

Income and Net worth

The YouTuber and social media influencer is worth a total of $20 million. YouTube, in particular, contributes significantly to his profits. By November 2019, he was earning $275k per month only from ad revenue. Meanwhile, David’s empire, which includes business sponsorships from companies like EA, Bumble, and SeatGeek, earned $16 million in 2020. Furthermore, his clothing company, Clickbait, which sells hoodies, shorts, trousers, and other goods, accounts for a significant portion of his yearly earnings.

Lifestyle

So, how does this very successful YouTuber spend his time? Surprisingly, when Dobrik graduated from high school, his parents offered him the choice of attending college or moving out. Dobrik and a few friends went to Los Angeles in 2013, and the group battled to survive on cheap items like peanut butter and bread. But the tables have entirely flipped!

David is most known for being the leader of the famous YouTube group The Vlog Squad, which features regularly in his vlogs and is made up of a rotating group of his pals. The Vlog Squad includes Jason Nash, Natalie Mariduena, Josh Peck, Zane Hijazi, and Heath Hussar. Furthermore, Dobrik is a major Iron Man enthusiast who has a $443,00 Iron Man outfit. Also, one of David’s favorite pleasures is partying with friends and recording them when drunk, which usually ends up being hilarious. The social media phenomenon is well-known for his out-of-this-world pranks on his friends, such as marrying his friends’ moms and posing as police to arrest them at a party.

Charity

If you know David, you are aware of how much he likes assisting people. Dobrik collaborated with HeadCount to give 5 fans the chance to win one of Tesla’s three automobiles in order to increase voter registration on the HeadCount website. Surprisingly, this alliance has surpassed its goal of 200,000 registered voters for the 2020 election by 300,000. David Dobrik is also well-known for his giving presents to friends, family, and even random people on the street.

However, one fan’s life was permanently changed when he gave $25,000 to assist her get through a tough time. In addition, during the COVID-19 pandemic in April, the YouTuber employed a tee-shirt cannon to blast $21,000 checks to fans in need of financial assistance, making it one of his biggest giveaways ever. Earlier this year, he donated $25,000 to a single mother and another DACA recipient. In addition, in the aftermath of the police killing of George Floyd, YouTube celebrity David Dobrik donated nearly $50,000 to nonprofits supporting the Black Lives Matter movement. He not only handed his viewers iPads, PS4s, Xboxes, and frisbees with money taped to the bottoms, but he also gave away a large number of new cars. He also offered a few fortunate spectators $10,000 cheques.

David Dobrik Career

Dobrik’s first Vine post was in 2013, on the now-defunct short-form video-sharing program Vine. Furthermore, his six-second video was a hit, and he amassed a sizable Vine following of over a million people. He was also a member of the YouTube group Second Class, which had over 18,000 subscribers until closing down in 2015. Dobrik has also appeared as a judge on the Nickelodeon TV program America’s Most Musical Family and voiced characters in The Angry Birds Movie 2. He’s also hosted a SpongeBob SquarePants special and the first season of Discovery Channel’s reality competition program Dodgeball Thunderdome.
