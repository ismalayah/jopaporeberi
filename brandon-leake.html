Brandon Leake Biography

Brandon Leake was born on May 4, 1992, in Los Angeles, California. He is currently 31 years old, and his zodiac sign is Taurus. Tyrone and Carla Leake-Gibson are his parents. Leake was reared by a single mother in Stockton, California, who worked 15 different jobs to make ends meet.

Furthermore, when his younger sister Danielle Marie Gibson died in 1997, he was just four years old. At the time, Danielle was only 8 months old. He met his biological father for the first time on August 31, 2017. Leake wanted to attempt martial arts as a kid, but after seeing a friend knock out a kid and break his nose in a karate competition, he realized it wasn’t for him. The focus of this personality then shifted to basketball. Brandon went to Stockton’s Commodore Stockton Skills School. He began writing poetry there. After college, he worked on an All-America City piece with Stockton Poet Laureate Tama Brisbane, which revived his enthusiasm for poetry.

Later, he attended Stockton Edison High School and Redding, California’s Simpson University. In addition, he was an English teacher at Edison High School. Brandon began his teaching career at Able Charter Schools in Stockton before moving on to Delta College in Stockton as an academic counselor.

As a freshman on Simpson University’s NCAA Division II basketball team, Leake concentrated on basketball. Called To Move was created in 2012 by him and a small student poetry club at the institution. As a result, it grew into an organization that allowed him to inspire others through poetry all around the United States. And, in April 2014, this individual received his bachelor’s degree from Simpson University, giving him the family’s first college graduate. Later that year, in Sacramento, California, he joined a slam poetry team.

Career

This individual launched his debut album, “In My Thoughts,” in 2016, and organized a performance tour for himself, performing 40 gigs in 12 states. Similarly, when he auditioned for Season 12 of “America’s Got Talent” in 2017, he was rejected by the show’s screeners and his audition was not shown.

Brandon, on the other hand, was crowned the winner of the 15th season in September 2020. This winner receives a $1 million cash prize, a brand new Kia Stinger, and the chance to appear on the Luxor Las Vegas stage. With his Dark Side Tour, Leake has performed in 36 US states, Mexico, New Zealand, and Canada to promote his published poems and music, Deficiencies: A Tale from My Dark Side.

Brandon is the founder and CEO of Called to Move, a non-profit that uses poetry to help young people. He also works at San Joaquin Delta College as an academic advisor. Furthermore, on October 9, 2020, United Talent Agency revealed that this individual has signed with them. He will also act as a producing partner and contributor to the Team Harmony Foundation’s new worldwide online series, ‘HATE: WHAT ARE YOU GOING TO DO?’, which will premiere in October 2020.
